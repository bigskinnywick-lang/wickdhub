Part 1~5-Minute Self-Introduction

The agenda asks each member to cover who you are / the area you support and your primary responsibilities. Below is a speakable talking track (~5 min), with a tight 2-minute fallback at the end.

Opening — who you are

  • "I'm Adam Wick — I'm the Call Center Trainer on the Customer Care team."
  • I own training for our contact-center agents: the people on the phones with carriers every day.

The area you support

  • My world is the agent experience — taking a new hire from day one to confidently handling live calls on PrePass products and services.
  • I support the full product surface agents touch: weigh-station bypass, devices & transponders (incl. ElitePass 2.0), tolls, INFORM safety scores, account servicing, and billing — and the systems they live in: Dynamics 365, CSM, Five9, and our Knowledge Base.

Primary responsibilities

  • I design and deliver our 30-day New Hire program — ~27 facilitator-guide modules across Customer Service and Billing, plus the Day-1 Intro to PrePass and onboarding assessments.
  • I'm moving us from instructor-led training to self-paced eLearning — I build in Articulate (Rise & Storyline) and publish through Cornerstone. Several are live, like the Verification module and an end-of-call documentation mini-module.
  • I'm one of six co-owners of our D365 Knowledge Base, keeping what agents reference on a call aligned with what we teach them.

Why I'm on the council

  • I sit where product knowledge becomes agent behavior — so I see firsthand where training is consistent, where it's thin, and where it's gone stale.
  • I'm especially interested in reducing duplication and making sure the message a customer hears from an agent matches what they get everywhere else.
  • Optional warm close: "I'm a Learner and an Ideation person by nature — I like figuring out how things work and finding better ways to teach them."

Part 2Inventory-Debrief Talking Points

Mapped to the four debrief questions on the agenda.

Q1 — Where is there consistency, and where are the gaps?

  • Strong & current: the agent-facing core (bypass, devices, tolls, account servicing, billing, call handling) was rebuilt this spring (FG v3, March 2026) — consistent and recent.
  • Gaps with no training: Technical Support (none yet), 360 SmartView, ElitePass 2.0 (KB FAQ only — agents still trained on generic "device types"), new app-only network sites (Inkom, Clarion, Floyd County), and Geotab / TSP integrations.
  • Where the unowned reference lives: for Tech Support and TSP integrations the reference today is the Mobile App Product Central SharePoint site, owned by Abhi Reddy — a good source the council could build formal training from.

Q2 — Multiple teams on the same product — same message?

  • Biggest overlap: training content vs. the Knowledge Base (co-owned by six of us) — worth confirming the KB and facilitator guides tell agents the same thing.
  • Open question: who else builds training or customer-facing material on devices/ElitePass, tolls, and safety scores? If product, marketing, or customer success run their own versions, we should check the agent message matches what customers see.

Q3 — Minimal training & underserved audiences

  • Minimal training: Technical Support (none), 360 SmartView, ElitePass 2.0 (FAQ only), Geotab/TSP.
  • Most underserved audience: customers. Almost everything I own is agent-facing; the only customer-facing piece is the PrePass Mobile / My PrePass walk-through.

Q4 — How much is outdated or inaccessible?

  • Outdated: the D365 Customer Service Hub → Workspace switch happened on the floor, but training materials weren't fully updated — a known content gap.
  • Mostly current otherwise: FG v3 modules date to March 2026; the Intro/NEO deck (Dec 2025) is the oldest core asset.
  • Accessibility: content is split between SharePoint/local FG masters and Cornerstone/Reach360. As more converts to Articulate/Cornerstone, accessibility improves — worth raising whether the council wants a single shared repository (the Teams channel could anchor it).

One synthesis point to leave them with close

My inventory shows a deep, recently-refreshed agent-training library with two soft spots: newer products (ElitePass 2.0, SmartView, Geotab/TSP) lag behind, and customer-facing self-serve training is thin. Those two themes are strong candidates for where the council invests first.
