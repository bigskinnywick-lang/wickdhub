Where It All Started (1980s–1995)

~3 min · 3 blocks
1.1
The Problem on the Highway
TextDesign
▼
Narration Script
In the 1980s, every commercial truck on American highways had to stop at weigh stations — every single time. Safe, compliant carriers sat in the same lines as everyone else. The delays cost the trucking industry billions in fuel, time, and productivity. State DOT directors and trucking executives in western states asked a simple question: What if safe trucks could keep moving?

1.2
The Crescent Project
TextAvailable
▼
Narration Script
That question launched the Crescent Project in the late 1980s — a collaborative research effort between state DOTs and trucking executives across Arizona, California, New Mexico, Oregon, Texas, Washington, and British Columbia. The goal: test technologies for weighing and screening trucks at highway speeds without stopping them. In 1991, the project evaluated multiple approaches and chose RFID — Radio Frequency Identification — as the winning technology. That decision is still at the heart of what we do today.

1.3
HELP Inc. Is Born
TextAvailable
▼
Narration Script
In 1993, the research became an organization. HELP Inc. — Heavy-vehicle Electronic License Plate, Inc. — was chartered as a 501(c)(3) nonprofit by trucking industry leaders, enforcement agencies, and state DOTs. The structure was intentional: a nonprofit where the trucking industry and government agencies shared equal seats at the table. Lockheed Martin was contracted to build the system, and in 1995, PrePass went live at its first weigh station sites in California.

Growth, Change & the CVO Pivot (1995–2022)

~4 min · 4 blocks
2.1
The Technology Kept Moving
TextDesign
▼
Narration Script
For decades, PrePass expanded steadily across the country. But the technology operations behind the scenes changed hands multiple times — from Lockheed Martin to Xerox, from Xerox to Conduent. Each transition meant adapting to a new corporate owner running the day-to-day technology while HELP Inc. continued to own the mission and the network.

CVO Holding Company & Alinda Capital

For the first time, the technology vendor was purpose-built to serve PrePass rather than being a division inside a giant corporation. This set the stage for what came next.

2.4
The Pivotal Moment — June 2022
TextAvailable
▼
Narration Script
June 2022 was the turning point. PrePass Safety Alliance acquired CVO Holding Company, bringing the technology operations fully in-house for the first time in the organization’s history. No more third-party vendors running the platform. CVO was rebranded as PrePass LLC in January 2023 with Chris Murray named President. This single move transformed PrePass from a nonprofit that outsourced its technology to a nonprofit that owns and operates everything — the mission, the network, the platform, and the people.

PrePass Today

~3 min · 4 blocks
3.1
By the Numbers
TextDesign
▼
Narration Script
Today, PrePass is the most utilized weigh station bypass and toll management platform in North America. The numbers tell the story: 584+ bypass locations across 46 jurisdictions. Over 100,000 motor carriers and 800,000+ commercial vehicles enrolled. More than 1 billion cumulative safe bypasses facilitated. An estimated $9 billion saved for the trucking industry and 405 million gallons of fuel conserved. And $193 million in annual revenue — entirely self-funded, with zero charitable contributions.

3.2
More Than Bypass
TextDesign
▼
Narration Script
PrePass started as a weigh station bypass provider, but that’s only part of the picture today. The product portfolio has expanded into a multi-product fleet platform. Bypass is the foundation — both RFID transponder and mobile app. PrePass Plus combines bypass and toll payment on a single E-ZPass compatible device. Toll Management provides nationwide consolidated billing, dispute resolution, and GPS toll verification through INFORM Tolling. INFORM Safety delivers data analytics for ISS and CSA safety score improvement. And the 2025 acquisition of FleetDrive 360 added AI-driven FMCSA and DOT compliance.

3.3
Where We’re Headed
TextDesign
▼
Narration Script
PrePass is executing a platform expansion strategy — evolving from a single-product bypass provider into the trucking industry’s comprehensive operations platform. Recent moves include the FleetDrive 360 acquisition for AI-driven compliance, the NECS Fleet Solutions partnership for vehicle legalization and fuel tax recovery, the launch of AI Toll Insights for machine-learning toll analytics, geographic expansion into Maine and Vermont, and deeper integrations with fleet tech platforms like Motive, Samsara, and Geotab. The vision: one trusted platform for bypass, tolling, compliance, safety, and analytics.

3.4
The Competitive Landscape
TextDesign
▼
Narration Script
You should know that PrePass doesn’t operate in a vacuum. In August 2024, Bestpass-Fleetworthy Solutions acquired Drivewyze and rebranded as Fleetworthy — creating a unified competitor with bypass, toll management, and compliance services. What makes PrePass different? We’re the only provider offering both RFID transponder and mobile app bypass. The only system combining bypass and toll payment on one device. The only preclearance system developed, owned, and operated in the United States as a nonprofit. And we maintain 99.99% RFID transmission reliability with stronger data privacy — carrier data is purged after 90 days.
