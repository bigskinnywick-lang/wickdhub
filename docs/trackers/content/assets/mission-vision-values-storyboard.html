Mission in Action — What This Looks Like
CONFIRMED CONTENT
LABELED GRAPHIC or PROCESS BLOCK
On-Screen Text

Every time a truck approaches a PrePass-equipped site:

1. An RFID transponder signal is read
2. Data is cross-referenced against safety and credentials databases in real time
3. The driver gets a green light (bypass) or red light (pull in) — in under 3 seconds
4. Safe carriers move faster. Unsafe carriers get inspected. Everyone is safer.

This is the mission, live, millions of times a day across 34+ states.

Visual Suggestion

Rise "Process" or "Labeled Graphic" block — a simple 4-step flow showing the bypass in action. If you have the stock photo of a weigh station or PrePass sign, use it as the background. This makes the mission concrete before moving to vision.

Reflection — Which Value Resonates?
LEARNER INTERACTION — KEPT FROM REV 1
KNOWLEDGE CHECK BLOCK (SURVEY MODE)
Interaction Design

Type: Reflection poll (ungraded) — Rise "Knowledge Check" set to survey mode, or a "Sorting" interaction.

Prompt: "Think about your own experience — in a previous role, in school, or in your personal life. Which of these values resonates most with you right now?"

○ Our mission drives us
○ We invest in relationships
○ We challenge ourselves to innovate and improve
○ We win together

Feedback (all answers): "There's no wrong answer here — every one of these values plays a role in your work at PrePass. As you settle into your role, you'll see them show up in meetings, project decisions, and how your team operates. Pay attention to which ones come up most."

Builder Note

This is intentionally low-stakes — the goal is engagement, not assessment. It gives learners a moment to pause and connect personally before the graded knowledge check. In Rise, use a "Knowledge Check" block with survey/practice mode (no score tracking).

Correct: "Nice work! You've got a solid understanding of what each value means in practice."

Incorrect: "Not quite — take another look. Each value has a distinct focus: mission alignment, relationship building, innovation, or collaboration. Try again!"

Builder Note

This knowledge check tests whether learners can distinguish between the four values — not just recall their names. The descriptions are drawn from the NEO's expanded paragraphs but shortened to work as matching items. In Rise, use "Knowledge Check" block set to graded mode with "Matching" question type.
