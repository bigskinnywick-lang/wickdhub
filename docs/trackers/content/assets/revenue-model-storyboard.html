EBITDA: Earnings Before Interest, Taxes, Depreciation, and Amortization

What is it? EBITDA strips out financial factors that don’t reflect day-to-day operational performance — things like interest payments, tax obligations, and the accounting treatment of long-term assets. What’s left is a clearer picture of how well the core business is performing.

Why does PrePass use it? Because it focuses on operational results. When leadership talks about “hitting EBITDA targets,” they’re measuring whether the business is generating healthy revenue from its services, managing its costs effectively, and sustaining the model that funds our mission.

Why should you care? EBITDA is a key performance metric at PrePass. You’ll hear it referenced in all-hands meetings, financial updates, and goal-setting conversations. Understanding what it means helps you follow how the company measures success.

Voiceover Script

“You’ll hear a term at PrePass that might be unfamiliar: EBITDA. It stands for Earnings Before Interest, Taxes, Depreciation, and Amortization. It sounds complicated, but the concept is simple. EBITDA strips out financial factors that don’t reflect daily operations — like interest payments and how we account for long-term assets. What’s left is a clean measure of how well the core business is performing. When leadership talks about hitting EBITDA targets, they’re asking: are we generating healthy revenue, managing costs well, and sustaining the model that funds our mission? You don’t need to calculate it, but you should know what it means when you hear it.”
