The Four Pillars — What the Alliance Stands For
CONFIRMED CONTENT — NEW IN REV 2
TABS / ACCORDION BLOCK
On-Screen — Interactive Pillar Cards

Everything the Alliance does is built on four pillars:

Safety
Electronically screening and bypassing the safest fleets reduces congestion at weigh stations and focuses agency resources on at-risk carriers. Every bypass of a safe truck makes the road safer for everyone.
Advocacy
Collaboration between the Alliance, government agencies, and trucking companies since inception. PrePass brings both sides to the same table — literally, on the board — to advance shared goals.
Efficiency
Save Time, Fuel, and Money for carriers while making regulatory compliance more efficient. Bypasses save an estimated 3.5 minutes per stop — multiplied across millions of transactions a year.
Innovation
Focusing on innovative technology and services for safety and decision-making. From RFID transponders in the 1990s to AI-driven compliance today, the Alliance continuously evolves how highways operate.
Voiceover Script

The Alliance organizes its mission around four pillars — and each one connects directly to the work PrePass does every day.

Safety is the foundation. By electronically screening trucks at highway speed, PrePass identifies which fleets have the strongest safety records and lets them bypass. That frees up state inspectors to focus their limited time on carriers that actually need attention. It’s a smarter use of everyone’s resources.

Advocacy is about partnership. The Alliance was built from day one as a collaboration between government agencies and the trucking industry. That’s not just a talking point — it’s baked into the governance structure, which we’ll see in a moment.

Efficiency is the promise to carriers. Every bypass saves time, fuel, and money. When you multiply that across hundreds of thousands of trucks and millions of transactions, the economic impact is enormous.

Innovation is how the Alliance stays relevant. PrePass started with RFID technology in the early 1990s. Today it’s expanding into AI-powered compliance, GPS toll verification, and data analytics. The technology changes; the commitment to safety doesn’t.

Rise “Tabs” or “Accordion” block — one tab per pillar. Learner clicks each tab to reveal the pillar description and real-world example. Consider an icon for each pillar (shield for Safety, handshake for Advocacy, clock/fuel gauge for Efficiency, lightbulb for Innovation). Ensure all four tabs must be visited before the learner can proceed (Rise “Continue” button gating).

Voiceover Script

Now that you understand why the Alliance exists, let’s look at how it’s organized to deliver on that mission.

At the top is PrePass Safety Alliance — the 501(c)(3) nonprofit parent. The Alliance owns the PrePass brand, sets the strategic direction, and manages relationships with state agencies across the country. Its board has equal representation from government officials and trucking industry leaders — neither side can outvote the other, and nobody on the board gets paid. That balance is what gives PrePass credibility with both regulators and the carriers it serves.

Below the Alliance is PrePass LLC, the wholly-owned commercial subsidiary. This is where you work. PrePass LLC builds and runs all the technology — bypass, tolling, compliance, analytics — everything carriers interact with. It’s led by President Chris Murray, who reports to Alliance CEO Mark Doughty.

There’s also a third entity called Auxilium, Inc. that handles specific government contracts where the Alliance would otherwise have a conflict of interest. You don’t need to know the details right now — just know it exists to keep governance clean.

KNOWLEDGE CHECK BLOCK
Knowledge Check — 5 Questions

Q1: What is the primary purpose of PrePass Safety Alliance?

(A) To generate profit for shareholders through transportation services
(B) To serve as a nonprofit public-private partnership dedicated to highway safety
(C) To enforce federal trucking regulations on behalf of the government
(D) To compete with other bypass providers in the private market

Correct: B — The Alliance is a 501(c)(3) nonprofit partnership of state agencies and trucking industry leaders. (NEO slide 4)

Q2: Which of the following is NOT one of the Alliance’s four pillars?

(A) Safety
(B) Profitability
(C) Efficiency
(D) Innovation

Correct: B — The four pillars are Safety, Advocacy, Efficiency, and Innovation. Profitability is not a pillar of a nonprofit organization. (NEO slides 5–6)

Q3: How much has the Alliance invested to date on highway safety services?

(A) Over $100 million
(B) Over $500 million
(C) Over $800 million
(D) Over $2 billion

Correct: C — “To date, the Alliance has invested over $800 million on services that improve safety, reduce truck-related emissions and preserve highway infrastructure.” (NEO slide 5 speaker notes)

Q4: Who does PrePass LLC President Chris Murray report to?

(A) The PrePass LLC board of directors
(B) The American Trucking Association
(C) Alliance CEO Mark Doughty
(D) The U.S. Department of Transportation

Correct: C — PrePass LLC is a wholly-owned subsidiary of the Alliance. Chris Murray reports to Mark Doughty. (NEO slide 34, intelligence report)

Q5: What makes the Alliance board structure unique?

(A) All board members are paid executives from the trucking industry
(B) The board is appointed by the federal government
(C) It has equal representation from public-sector officials and private-sector trucking leaders, with no compensation
(D) Board seats are purchased by the highest bidder

Correct: C — The board balances government officials (state DOTs, highway patrol) and industry leaders (fleet owners, trucking associations). Neither side can outvote the other, and members serve without pay. (Intelligence report)

Builder Note

Quiz-style for Awareness depth. Questions cover all three learning objectives: purpose/pillars (Q1, Q2), proof points (Q3), and structure (Q4, Q5). Distractors are plausible but clearly wrong to avoid trick-question frustration at the awareness level. Rise Knowledge Check block with immediate feedback after each question.
