Course Purpose & Audience

Audience: Customer Service agents (inbound/outbound call center).

What this course does: Makes CS agents confident in talking about inForm Safety — what it is, why carriers love it, and how to introduce it to customers. This is a product awareness + talk-track course, not a troubleshooting course.

What it does NOT do: Set up access, read dashboard data in depth, troubleshoot subscriptions, or explain data refresh mechanics. Those skills belong to the inForm Safety — Tech Support course (companion build).

Hand-off rule baked into this course: If the conversation goes beyond introduction or interest, CS routes to Tech Support. No intake, no setup, no data debugging.

Tool: Articulate 360 (Rise recommended — content-heavy, light interactions).

1.2
Why carriers care — ISS score & BASICs at a glance
VOSSHAVE
▾
Voiceover Script (~60s)
Two terms show up constantly on these calls: ISS score and BASICs. You don't need to be an expert on either — but here's what you need to recognize. The ISS score, set by FMCSA, decides if a truck bypasses a weigh station or gets pulled in. Lower is better. The BASICs are the seven safety categories FMCSA uses to calculate that score — things like Unsafe Driving, Hours-of-Service Compliance, Vehicle Maintenance, and so on. Roadside inspections and violations feed into those categories on a rolling 24-month window. Carriers care about inForm because it shows them all of this in one place — instead of chasing data across government websites.
Visual
Left side: Simple definition card for ISS ("Inspection Selection System — FMCSA score, 0–100, lower = more bypass eligibility").
Right side: BASICs bar chart from the marketing PDF — the 7-category visual. Resources/InformSAFETY.pdf Available
Language Note Agents don't need to memorize all 7 BASICs — just recognize the term. If a customer says "my vehicle maintenance BASIC is high," the agent should recognize that's a real thing in inForm and route accordingly.

When & How to Bring It Up

3 slides · ~2.5 min
3.1
Listen for these cues
VOBUILD
▾
Voiceover Script (~45s)
You're not a salesperson — but you are in a great position to mention inForm when a customer gives you a natural opening. Listen for: questions about safety scores, complaints about weigh station pull-ins, mentions of inspections or violations, or any frustration about tracking safety data. When you hear those, you've got a cue. A quick, friendly mention is often all it takes.
Design Note Rise "Flashcards" or "Labeled Graphic" interaction — 4 cards, each showing a customer phrase and the cue it represents.
Content
Card 1: "Why do I keep getting pulled into weigh stations?" → Cue: ISS score / bypass issue.
Card 2: "How do I see my inspection history?" → Cue: they need the Data Insights view.
Card 3: "I don't know what's affecting our safety score." → Cue: BASICs breakdown is exactly this.
Card 4: "Is there a way to get reports emailed to me?" → Cue: subscriptions. Design in Articulate
3.2
The 30-second intro (what to actually say)
VOBUILD
▾
Voiceover Script (~50s)
Here's a talk-track you can lift word-for-word. Use it when a customer gives you a cue — or any time it feels natural. "Since you're asking about [their concern], I want to make sure you know about inForm Safety. It's a free dashboard that comes with your PrePass subscription — it shows your ISS score, your inspection history, and what's driving your safety numbers, all in one place. If you're interested, I can connect you with our team that sets up access. Want me to go ahead?" That's the whole script. Short, clear, and it ends with a question that makes the hand-off easy.
Design Note Rise "Process" block or Quote block styled as a script card. Highlight the question at the end in a different color.
Talk-Track Nugget The close: "Want me to go ahead?" — this sentence is what turns the intro into an action. If they say yes, you warm-transfer. If they say no, no pressure, you move on.
3.3
Handling "tell me more" — keep it short, then hand off
VOBUILD
▾
Voiceover Script (~40s)
If a customer says, "tell me more," don't try to be the expert. Hit the three features you already know: the Company Snapshot dashboard, the visual heat maps, and the automated email reports. Then transition to the hand-off: "The best way to see it is to have our team get you set up — I'll connect you with them." Your job ends at interest. The moment it turns into "how do I log in" or "my data looks wrong," it's a Tech Support conversation.
Do NOT • Don't walk the customer through the login page.
• Don't explain data refresh timing or why numbers differ between inForm and FMCSA websites.
• Don't collect email/name/DOT for setup — that workflow belongs to Tech Support now.
• Don't quote subscription limits or subscription format. You can mention they exist; Tech Support explains the mechanics.

The Hand-Off

1 slide · ~1 min
4.1
Where inForm calls go
VOBUILD
▾
Voiceover Script (~55s)
Three kinds of inForm calls, three routes. If the customer is an existing PrePass account and wants access — warm-transfer to Tech Support, they handle the setup end to end. If the customer is already using inForm and has a data question or something's not working — same thing, Tech Support. And if the caller isn't a PrePass customer at all but wants inForm, that's an Inside Sales call — English or Spanish line depending on the caller. The common thread: CS introduces the product, Tech Support and Sales do the heavy lifting.

Knowledge Check

3 questions · ~1.5 min
5.1
Q1 — The one-sentence pitch
QUIZ
▾
Question (Multiple Choice)
A customer asks, "What even is inForm Safety?" — which response is best?

A) "It's a premium add-on that costs extra but it's really worth it." ❌
B) "It's a free online dashboard that shows what's driving your ISS score." ✅
C) "It's a tool our engineers use to track your account." ❌
D) "Honestly I'd have to transfer you for that one." ❌
Feedback (correct) Exactly right. Free, online, dashboard, ISS score — four ideas, one sentence. That's the core pitch.
Feedback (incorrect) inForm Safety is free with PrePass and PrePass Plus — and you can absolutely answer a basic "what is it" question without a transfer. Save the transfer for setup and data questions.
5.2
Q2 — Spotting the cue
QUIZ
▾
Question (Multiple Choice)
You're on a call and the customer says, "I feel like my trucks are getting pulled into weigh stations way more than they used to." What's a natural next move?

A) Apologize and escalate to a supervisor. ❌
B) Mention inForm Safety — weigh station pull-ins connect directly to ISS score, which inForm visualizes. ✅
C) Tell them weigh stations are operated by the states, nothing you can do. ❌
D) Offer them a refund. ❌
Feedback (correct) Nice catch. Weigh station frustration is one of the clearest cues that inForm can help — because ISS score is the thing driving it.
5.3
Q3 — Where the line is
QUIZ
▾
Question (Multiple Choice)
The customer says, "Great, sign me up — and also my HOS data in inForm looks wrong." What do you do?

A) Collect their email, name, and DOT and email inform@prepass.com. ❌
B) Tell them inForm data is always correct and not to worry. ❌
C) Warm-transfer to Tech Support — both setup and data questions belong there. ✅
D) Offer to log in on their behalf and look at the data. ❌
Feedback (correct) Correct. In the new model, CS introduces and hands off. Setup and data questions both live with Tech Support — one clean transfer.
Feedback (incorrect) Collecting email/name/DOT and emailing inform@prepass.com used to be the CS workflow — it isn't anymore. That intake now lives with Tech Support. Your role stops at interest.
