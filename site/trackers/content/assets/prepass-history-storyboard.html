"The PrePass Story — From Research Project to Industry Standard"
Estimated time: 10–12 minutes  |  Learning depth: Awareness

PrePass isn't just the name on your badge — it's a 30-year mission to make America's highways safer. In 1993, the research became an organization. HELP Inc. — Heavy-vehicle Electronic License Plate, Inc. — was chartered as a 501(c)(3) nonprofit by trucking industry leaders, enforcement agencies, and state DOTs.

Part C — Corporate Ownership Journey (1993–2022)
6
The Ownership Sequence
CONFIRMED CONTENT — CORRECTED IN REV 2
PROCESS / TIMELINE BLOCK
On-Screen Text

For decades, PrePass expanded steadily across the country. But the technology operations behind the scenes changed hands multiple times:

Lockheed Martin (1993) — Built and initially operated the system
ACS (Affiliated Computer Services) (2001) — Acquired Lockheed Martin's transportation services division
Xerox (2010) — Acquired ACS, inherited PrePass operations
Conduent (2017) — Spun off from Xerox as a standalone business process company
CVO Holding Company / Alinda Capital (2018) — Purpose-built entity to serve PrePass

Through every transition, HELP Inc. (later PrePass Safety Alliance) continued to own the mission and the network. The technology vendor changed; the purpose never did.

Voiceover Script

While the PrePass network kept growing, the technology operations changed hands multiple times. Lockheed Martin built the original system. In 2001, ACS — Affiliated Computer Services — acquired Lockheed Martin's transportation services. In 2010, Xerox acquired ACS. In 2017, Xerox spun off Conduent as a standalone company. And in 2018, HELP Inc. partnered with Alinda Capital Partners to create CVO Holding Company — a purpose-built vendor for PrePass. Through every transition, the nonprofit parent continued to own the mission. The technology vendor changed; the purpose never did.

Part D — Product & Service Evolution (Dual Track)
9
While the Ownership Changed, the Products Kept Growing
CONFIRMED CONTENT — NEW IN REV 2
TEXT + TIMELINE BLOCK
On-Screen Text

While corporate ownership shifted, PrePass never stopped innovating. Here's how the product portfolio evolved:

1989 — Crescent Project: research begins
1993 — PrePass weigh station bypass: the original service
2003 — PrePass Plus: adds toll payment to the bypass transponder
2013 — Carrier Portal: self-service tools for fleet managers
2015 — ElitePass: premium service tier
2018 — MOTION mobile app: bypass without hardware
2019 — ALERTS: safety and compliance notifications
2023–24 — TSP integrations: connecting with Motive, Samsara, Geotab
2025 — FleetDrive 360: AI-driven FMCSA/DOT compliance platform (acquisition)

From one product to a comprehensive fleet operations platform — each milestone built on the one before it.

Voiceover Script

While the corporate owners changed, the products never stopped evolving. PrePass started as a weigh station bypass service in 1993. By 2003, PrePass Plus added toll payment to the same transponder. In 2013, the Carrier Portal gave fleet managers self-service tools. ElitePass launched in 2015 as a premium tier. The MOTION mobile app arrived in 2018, letting drivers bypass weigh stations without any hardware. ALERTS added safety notifications in 2019. Then came TSP integrations in 2023 and 2024, connecting PrePass with fleet management platforms like Motive and Samsara. And in 2025, the acquisition of FleetDrive 360 brought AI-driven compliance into the ecosystem. That's the arc — from one product to a comprehensive fleet platform.
